Noun Prefixes

Also known as: cvi

Introduction

In the last lesson, we saw that Sanskrit allows us to change nouns into verb roots. We learned one method for doing so: turning the noun stem into a verb root. In this lesson, we will see that we can also turn nouns into verb prefixes:

  • स्वागतीकृत्य

    svāgatīkṛtya

    Having done a welcome (i.e. "having welcomed"), … Meghaduta

The basics

Nouns usually become prefixes to two verbs: bhū and kṛ. If the noun ends in a, its final vowel is usually changed to ī. The meaning of the verb depends on the root used:

Root
Meaning
bhū
become X
kṛ
do or perform X

The use and meaning of these "noun prefixes" are both quite straightforward, so let's look at just one more example before moving on:

  • स्वल्पीभूते सुचरितफले स्वर्गिणाम् …

    svalpībhūte sucaritaphale svargiṇām …

    When the heaven-dwellers' good deeds have become meager … Meghaduta
